# Seyed Milad Hatamlee
Ph.D. Candidate in Mechanical Engineering  
University at Buffalo

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University at Buffalo, specializing in molecular simulations of fluid transport and phase behavior in nanoconfined systems. My research focuses on understanding the fundamental mechanisms governing CO₂ transport, water flow in nanochannels, and thermodynamic behavior in porous media relevant to energy applications.

My work integrates molecular dynamics (MD), Monte Carlo (GCMC and GEMC), and thermodynamic modeling using modified equations of state to develop predictive insights into nanoscale transport and phase equilibrium. I am particularly interested in bridging molecular-level phenomena with continuum-scale behavior for applications in energy systems and subsurface engineering.

## Research Interests
- Molecular Dynamics (MD)
- Monte Carlo Simulation (GCMC, GEMC)
- Nanoconfined Fluids
- CO₂ Transport and Carbon Storage
- Phase Equilibrium and Thermodynamics
- Multiphase Transport in Porous Media
- Energy Systems and Subsurface Engineering
